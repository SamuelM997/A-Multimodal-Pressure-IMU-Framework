# A-Multimodal-Pressure-IMU-Framework

Prolonged seated activity often results in asymmetric upper-limb loading and reduced shoulder mobility, which can cumulatively lead to musculoskeletal discomfort and long-term functional impairment. Existing ergonomic monitoring solutions largely rely on global posture assessment or simple inactivity timers, failing to account for left–right imbalance, micro-movement behavior, and the temporal persistence of harmful postures. This project presents a bilateral asymmetric upper-limb load and immobility detection system with side-specific corrective feedback for seated users, designed to identify sustained unilateral strain through cross-domain sensor confirmation and adaptive risk modeling.

The proposed system integrates a sensor-embedded seating overlay with wearable inertial sensing nodes to perform real-time monitoring of upper-body posture and shoulder movement. Force-sensitive resistors embedded in the backrest region measure left and right upper-back pressure distribution, while wireless inertial measurement units worn on each shoulder capture joint orientation and micro-movement intensity. A central processing unit fuses pressure and motion data to compute bilateral differential metrics, enabling detection of asymmetric loading conditions rather than absolute posture deviations.

A key feature of the system is its contextual self-calibration mechanism, wherein an initial temporal baseline is established during the first seating interval to adapt to individual user posture and seating conditions. The system further incorporates a time-weighted risk accumulation model that differentiates between passive static loading and active movement-assisted loading. Persistent asymmetric conditions increment a side- specific fatigue score, while corrective movement dynamically accelerates recovery, reflecting physiological strain behavior more accurately than fixed-threshold timers.

Upon detection of sustained unilateral immobility and load imbalance, the system delivers localized haptic feedback exclusively on the affected side, prompting corrective micro-movements without interrupting the user’s task. By combining bilateral comparison, temporal persistence, and selective feedback routing, the proposed system offers a robust, non-invasive ergonomic monitoring solution suitable for long-term seated use. The design emphasizes adaptability, low cost, and extensibility, making it applicable across a wide range of seated environments without performing medical diagnosis or treatment
